# ✅ **MASTER GENERATION PROMPT (Copy/Paste Ready)**

**PROMPT START**

You are to generate a structured analysis of four character archetypes who all use “strategic disorganization” for different motives.  
The four archetypes are:

1. **The Detective (Columbo)** – A deceptively rumpled but razor‑sharp investigator who uses disorganization to lower defenses and uncover truth.  
2. **The Fraudster (Malicious Criminal)** – A manipulative predator who weaponizes chaos, charm, and lies to exploit victims.  
3. **The Survivor (Huckster Salesman)** – A desperate, chaotic hustler who uses disorganization as a survival mechanism to feed his family.  
4. **The Protegé (Stalker / Infiltrator)** – A boundary‑violating infiltrator who weaponizes eagerness, naivety, and “student energy” to gain proximity and access to a target.  
*(All descriptions must remain fictional, analytical, and non‑instructional.)*

---

## **TASK 1 — Generate the Initial Table**

Create a **markdown table** titled:  
**“The Four Faces of Strategic Disorganization”**

Columns:  
- Core Trait  
- The Detective (Columbo)  
- The Fraudster  
- The Survivor  
- The Protegé  

Rows should include the following traits (and may expand them if needed):  
- The Rumpled Look  
- Strategic Forgetfulness  
- The False Exit  
- Simulated Naivety  
- The Constant Apology  
- Hyper-Focus on Trivialities  
- The Core Motivation  

Each cell should describe **how that archetype uses the trait**, in 1–2 sentences, in a fictional and analytical tone.

---

## **TASK 2 — Generate the Full CSV Version**

Produce a **CSV document** with the following structure:

**Archetype Descriptions Section**  
- Archetype  
- Description  
(Include one row per archetype.)

**Main Table Section**  
Columns:  
- Category Number  
- Category Name  
- Scenario Description  
- Columbo Response  
- Fraudster Response  
- Survivor Response  
- Protegé Response  

Include **all categories** from the table above, plus any additional categories you generate (you may expand to 20–30 categories if needed).  
All entries must remain fictional, analytical, and non‑instructional.

Format the CSV with:  
- Quoted fields  
- Comma separators  
- No markdown formatting around the CSV block

---

## **TASK 3 — Suggest Optional Expansions (Option C)**

At the end of your output, include a section titled:  
**“Optional Additions for Further Expansion”**

Suggest additional archetypes that could be added to the framework, such as:

- **The Bureaucrat** – Uses procedural fog and paperwork chaos to maintain control.  
- **The Idealist** – Uses disorganization as a sign of creative brilliance and moral purity.  
- **The Cynic** – Uses disorganization to signal detachment and superiority.  
- **The Fixer** – Uses controlled chaos to manipulate outcomes behind the scenes.  
- **The Caretaker** – Uses disorganization to appear self-sacrificing and indispensable.  

Also suggest optional expansions such as:  
- Adding 50–100 more behavioral categories  
- Creating a narrative bible  
- Writing sample scenes  
- Generating psychological profiles  
- Creating a downloadable CSV or PDF  
- Adding a 5th, 6th, or 7th archetype  
- Turning the framework into a story engine or worldbuilding tool
